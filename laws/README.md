# LOGOS Laws

LOGOS Laws are not opinions.

A LOGOS Law is a principle that has been repeatedly supported by experiments, metrics and observed audience behavior.

At the foundation stage, all principles remain candidates until tested.

---

## Law Lifecycle

```text
Intuition
→ Hypothesis
→ Experiment
→ Repeated Evidence
→ Law Candidate
→ LOGOS Law
```

---

## Current Law Candidates

### LC-001 — The product is never the hero

Strong narratives perform better when the human transformation is central and the product supports it indirectly.

Status: `untested / philosophical candidate`

---

### LC-002 — Meaning must survive without the product

If a statement is meaningful even when the product is removed, it has the potential to become a cultural idea.

Status: `untested / philosophical candidate`

---

### LC-003 — Simple images carry deep meaning

Simple, familiar images can transport complex meaning more effectively than abstract explanation.

Status: `untested / philosophical candidate`

---

### LC-004 — Contradiction creates attention

A human contradiction creates stronger attention than a direct product claim.

Status: `untested / philosophical candidate`

---

## Promotion Criteria

A candidate may become a LOGOS Law only when:

1. it has been tested across multiple content pieces;
2. it shows repeated positive signal;
3. it remains valid across more than one format or context;
4. it can be stated clearly;
5. it improves future generation decisions.
