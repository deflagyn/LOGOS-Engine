# LOGOS Glossary

This glossary defines the core language of LOGOS Engine.

The goal is to make the system precise enough for automation and clear enough for creative work.

---

## LOGOS

A system for engineering meaning and structured belief change.

LOGOS does not start with a product.

LOGOS starts with a human contradiction.

---

## Meaning

A frame that makes information emotionally, socially and behaviorally significant.

Information says what something is.

Meaning explains why it matters.

---

## Human Truth

A stable observation about human behavior, fear, desire, identity or contradiction.

A Human Truth should feel recognizable.

Example:

> People naturally give more attention to what they see every day.

---

## Human Contradiction

A tension between what a person believes about themselves and how they act.

Example:

> I believe I care for myself, but I ignore parts of myself that are not visible.

Human Contradiction is the LOGOS equivalent of a TRIZ contradiction.

---

## Belief

A working assumption about the world, the self, others or the future.

Beliefs are not always stated directly.

They are often hidden inside habits.

---

## Belief Shift

The central operational unit of LOGOS.

A Belief Shift moves a person from an old belief to a new belief.

Format:

```text
Old Belief → New Belief
```

Example:

```text
Self-care is mainly visible care → True self-care does not end at the surface
```

---

## Meaning Atom

A compact, repeatable phrase or idea that carries a Belief Shift.

A strong Meaning Atom should survive without the product.

Example:

> True self-care does not end at the surface.

---

## Story Pattern

A reusable narrative structure that delivers a Meaning Atom.

It is not a script.

It is the skeleton behind many possible scripts.

---

## Emotional Result

The emotional state the audience should experience after encountering the content.

Examples:

- calm recognition;
- relief;
- dignity;
- responsibility without panic;
- courage;
- self-respect.

---

## Identity Shift

The new self-image that makes a behavior feel natural.

Example:

> I am a person who cares for myself completely.

Identity Shift is stronger than a direct command.

---

## Habit

A repeated behavior that becomes natural after a belief and identity shift.

In LOGOS, habit is not forced first.

It is made coherent through meaning.

---

## Product Role

The position of a product inside a story, habit or belief shift.

In LOGOS, the product is not the hero.

The product is a supporting instrument inside a human transformation.

---

## Runtime

A concrete application of LOGOS to one client, product, service, movement or category.

DeflaGyn is the first reference runtime.

LOGOS itself must remain universal.

---

## Experiment

A real-world test of a Human Truth, Belief Shift, Meaning Atom, Story Pattern or script.

An experiment must produce learning.

Views alone are not learning.

---

## Learning

A structured conclusion from an experiment.

Learning answers:

- what happened;
- why it may have happened;
- what changes next;
- whether the result supports a Law Candidate.

---

## Law Candidate

A principle that appears promising but does not yet have enough repeated evidence.

It must remain a candidate until tested multiple times.

---

## LOGOS Law

A validated principle of meaning, belief, story or behavior.

A LOGOS Law requires repeated evidence.

No idea becomes a law because it sounds beautiful.

---

## Meaning TRIZ

A LOGOS adaptation of TRIZ logic.

Classical TRIZ resolves contradictions in technical systems.

Meaning TRIZ resolves contradictions in meaning systems.

---

## Ideal Meaning Result

The state where a person arrives at the desired belief as if it were their own discovery.

This is the goal of strong stories, parables and cultural narratives.

---

## Meaning Resource

A value, desire, fear, identity or habit already present inside the audience.

LOGOS does not invent meaning resources.

It activates and reorganizes them.
