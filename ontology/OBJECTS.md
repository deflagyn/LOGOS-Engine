# LOGOS Ontology Objects

This document defines the core objects of LOGOS Engine.

The goal is to make meaning machine-readable without losing human depth.

---

## 1. Human Truth

A stable observation about human behavior, fear, desire, identity or contradiction.

Example:

> People often care more about what they can see every day than about what silently determines their future.

Human Truth is not a slogan. It is raw reality.

---

## 2. Human Contradiction

A tension between what a person believes about themselves and how they actually behave.

Example:

> A woman believes she cares for herself, but her care often stops at visible beauty.

This is the LOGOS equivalent of a TRIZ contradiction.

---

## 3. Belief Shift

The central LOGOS object.

A Belief Shift is a structured transition from an old belief to a new belief.

Format:

```text
Old Belief → New Belief
```

Example:

```text
Old Belief:
Self-care is mostly about visible appearance.

New Belief:
True self-care does not end at the surface.
```

---

## 4. Meaning Atom

A compact statement that carries the Belief Shift in a memorable form.

Example:

> True care for yourself does not end at the surface.

A Meaning Atom should be short, repeatable and able to live without the product.

---

## 5. Story Pattern

A reusable narrative structure that delivers a Meaning Atom.

Example:

```text
Visible routine → hidden contradiction → reframe → emotional resolution
```

---

## 6. Emotional Result

The emotional state the audience should experience after the story.

Examples:

- calm
- recognition
- dignity
- relief
- courage
- self-respect
- responsibility without panic

---

## 7. Identity Shift

The new self-image that makes the behavior natural.

Example:

> I am a woman who cares for herself completely.

Identity Shift is stronger than a call to action.

---

## 8. Habit

The repeated behavior that becomes natural after the belief and identity shift.

Example:

> Regular attention to women's health becomes part of self-care.

---

## 9. Product Role

The product is a supporting instrument inside the new habit.

It must not become the main hero of the story.

Example:

> DeflaGyn appears as one possible tool in a culture of attentive women's health care.

---

## 10. Experiment

A real-world test of a Belief Shift, Meaning Atom, Story Pattern or content format.

An experiment must include:

- hypothesis;
- platform;
- content version;
- metrics;
- learning;
- decision.

---

## 11. LOGOS Law

A principle that has been repeatedly supported by experiments.

A law cannot be created from intuition alone.

A law requires evidence from repeated tests.
